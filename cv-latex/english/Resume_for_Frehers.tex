\begin{rSection}{Project Experience}

{\bf Data Agent Training of LongCat 2.0\textsuperscript{‡} \quad \textit{Research Intern, LongCat Interaction}} \hfill {\em May 2026 -- Present} \\
To address the limitations of foundation models in data analysis, such as \textbf{insufficient drill-down} and \textbf{surface-level insights}, I design post-training strategies for \textbf{Data Agents} around \textbf{LongCat 2.0 Pro 1.6T}, with a focus on \textbf{Environment Scaling}, its downstream \textbf{trajectory-filtering pipeline}, and \textbf{mixing-ratio strategies} with other post-training data. The approach has been validated on \textbf{LongCat 2.0 Pro 1.6T} and adapted to a training stack spanning over \textbf{10,000 domestic H910-64G accelerators}, improving \textbf{drill-down ability} and \textbf{insight quality} on \textbf{Data Agent} tasks. \\

{\bf DeepRead: Document Structure-Aware Reasoning to Enhance Agentic Search\textsuperscript{†} \quad \textit{First Author}} \hfill {\em October 2025 -- February 2026} \\
Proposed \textbf{DeepRead}, a structure-aware document reasoning agent that preserves layout fidelity via OCR, constructs \textbf{paragraph-level coordinates}, and equips LLMs with \textbf{Retrieve} and \textbf{ReadSection} tools. Unlike flat chunk-based agentic search, DeepRead explicitly models document hierarchy and reading order, allowing the agent to first locate relevant regions and then inspect complete local context. The method induces a ``locate-then-read'' reasoning paradigm and improves over \textbf{Search-o1} style baselines by an average of \textbf{10.3\%} across four multi-type document benchmarks. \\
$\triangleright$~ \textbf{Resubmit to} \textit{KDD 2027 (CCF-A)}; the preprint was reported by the prominent technology media \textbf{New Wisdom}.

{\bf MuDABench: Multi-Document Analytical QA Benchmark and Multi-Agent Framework\textsuperscript{†} \quad \textit{First Author}} \hfill {\em April 2025 -- December 2025} \\
Constructed \textbf{MuDABench}, a multi-document analytical QA benchmark with \textbf{80k+ pages}, \textbf{332 questions}, and \textbf{4,964 structured intermediate facts}, to evaluate multi-document retrieval, cross-document aggregation, and numerical reasoning over large document collections. The benchmark uses structured intermediate facts to expose where a system fails: document selection, single-document extraction, cross-document aggregation, or final numerical reasoning. Designed a \textbf{Multi-Agent} workflow that substantially improves over standard retrieval baselines. \\
$\triangleright$~ \textbf{Published in} \textit{ACL 2026 (CCF-A)}.

{\bf SHAP-Based Causal Inference Framework for Corporate Finance\textsuperscript{*} \quad \textit{Project Leader}} \hfill {\em September 2024 -- January 2025} \\
Built an interpretable machine learning framework combining manually coded panel data and \textbf{Optuna} hyperparameter optimization, achieving $R^2=0.76$ on corporate total factor productivity prediction. Further used \textbf{SHAP} to quantify the \textbf{nonlinear marginal contribution} of ESG rating disagreement to outcome variables and reveal potential causal pathways linking heterogeneous ESG signals with productivity outcomes. \\
$\triangleright$~ \textbf{Published in} \textit{Finance Research Letters} (CAS Q1 Top, JCR Q1, IF: 6.9); received the Outstanding Paper Award at the Tsinghua Causal Inference Seminar.
